Research

Motion planning, robot learning, and autonomy under uncertainty.

My research work sits at the intersection of planning, prediction, control, and machine learning. I am especially interested in methods that turn uncertainty into an explicit design constraint for robots operating around other moving agents.

Featured Research

Receding horizon chance-constrained motion planning for uncertain multi-agent systems.

This project studies how autonomous agents can plan through cluttered environments while accounting for uncertainty in their own state, other agents, and moving obstacles. The implementation combines Chance-Constrained RRT tree expansion, priority-ordered trajectory prediction, Monte Carlo collision estimation, Kalman covariance updates, and receding-horizon replanning.

Chance Constraints Monte Carlo collision checks bound risk under uncertain state estimates.

Active Research

Adaptive localization for hidden targets using unknown-pose range-bearing beacons.

This project studies cooperative localization when a robot cannot directly observe the target and the assisting beacons are not globally calibrated. The estimator jointly recovers target position, beacon position, and beacon yaw while the robot motion supplies informative viewpoints for self-calibration.
